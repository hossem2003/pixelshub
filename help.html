Getting Started with Pixels

What is Pixels?

Pixels is an open-source collaborative learning platform where students create, improve, and share educational resources. Think of it as Wikipedia meets GitHub for learning materials.

How does it work?

  1. Browse - Explore courses, notes, and cheat sheets created by the community
  2. Learn - Study from high-quality, community-vetted resources
  3. Improve - Find something that could be better? Contribute your improvements!
  4. Earn Recognition - Get community score and badges for helpful contributions

Finding Resources

Use the Browse page to filter by subject, level, and content type. Resources are ranked by community interaction score, so the best versions rise to the top!

Contributing to Pixels

Creating a New Resource

  1. Click Create in the navigation
  2. Fill in the title, subject, and level
  3. Write your content using Markdown
  4. Publish to create version 1.0!

Tip: Start with a clear outline and add examples to make your resource more valuable.

Improving Existing Content

  1. Find a resource you want to improve
  2. Click Improve This Version
  3. Make your edits in the editor
  4. Describe what you changed and why
  5. Submit to create a new version!

Important: Always explain your changes! This helps the community understand your contribution.

What Makes a Good Contribution?

  • Fixes errors or adds missing information
  • Improves clarity and explanations
  • Adds helpful examples or visualizations
  • Updates outdated information
  • Makes unnecessary changes
  • Adds incorrect information
  • Copies content without attribution

 Understanding the Versioning System

How Versions Work

Every resource starts at version 1.0. When someone improves it, they create a new version (1.1, 1.2, etc.). Major revisions get a full version bump (2.0, 3.0).

Community Score

Each version earns a Community Score based on:

  • Upvotes from users
  • Number of people who view and use it
  • ¬ Positive discussion feedback
  •  Being used as a base for future versions

Higher-scored versions are shown first!

Version Comparison

Use the Compare Versions feature to see exactly what changed between any two versions. Changes are highlighted in green (additions), red (removals), and yellow (modifications).

Version History

View the complete timeline of improvements on the Versions page. You can see who contributed what and when!

Community Guidelines

Our Principles

  • Collaboration - We improve together, not compete
  •  Transparency - All changes are tracked and visible
  • Quality - Accuracy and clarity above all
  • Accessibility - Education should be free and open to everyone
  • – Respect - Be kind, constructive, and inclusive

Code of Conduct

  • Be respectful and professional
  • Provide constructive feedback
  • Give credit where it's due
  • Welcome newcomers
  • No harassment or discrimination
  • No spam or self-promotion
  • No plagiarism

Earning Badges

Contribute to the community and earn badges:

  • Top Contributor - 100+ community score
  • Version Master - Create 5 top-ranked versions
  • Knowledge Sharer - Publish 10 resources
  • Peer Reviewer - Review 20+ contributions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Pixels really free?

Yes! Pixels is completely free and open-source. We believe education should be accessible to everyone.

Do I need to create an account?

You can browse and view content without an account. Creating an account (free!) lets you contribute, earn badges, and track your learning.

What happens to my original content?

You're always credited as the original creator! Other users can improve your content, but you'll always be shown as the author of version 1.0.

Can I revert bad changes?

All versions are preserved! If a new version is worse, users can always access and use previous versions. The community score will naturally favor better versions.

How do I report inappropriate content?

Click the flag icon on any resource or discussion post. Our moderation team reviews all reports promptly.
